Lasagne

What makes Lasagne sooo good?

With ancient Greek origins, the modern, layered dish evolved in the Middle Ages in Italy. The early versions lacked tomatoes and béchamel, but the lasagne we know today, with its meat ragù and creamy sauce, was perfected in the late 19th century in Italy's Emilia-Romagna region.

The harmonious layering of textures and flavors helps keep this dish as a regular contender of perfection. Each bite combines tender pasta, rich meat sauce, and creamy béchamel, all tied together with melted cheese. This intricate balance of savory, creamy, and salty notes, combined with its status as a comforting, home-cooked meal, makes lasagne a truly satisfying culinary experience.

Ingredients (for 4 people):

For the Meat Sauce (Ragu):

  • Olive oil: 1 tbsp
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Carrot: 1 medium, finely diced
  • Celery: 1 stalk, finely diced
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Ground meat: 500g (a mix of beef and pork is excellent for flavor)
  • Tomato puree: 2 tbsp
  • Canned chopped tomatoes: 400g tin
  • Beef stock: 100-200ml
  • Dried herbs: 1 tsp of dried oregano or mixed Italian herbs
  • Salt and pepper: to taste

For the White Sauce (Bechamel):

  • Butter: 50g
  • Plain Flour: 50g
  • Milk: 550ml-600
  • Salt and white pepper: to taste
  • Nutmeg: a pinch, freshly grated if possible

For the Assembley:

  • Lasagne sheets: 9-12 sheets (depending on the size of your dish and how many layers you want)
  • Shredded Cheese: 150g of Mozarella, 50g of Parmesan

Method:

  1. Prepare the Ragu: Heat the olive oil in a large pan and cook the onion, carrot, and celery until soft, about 5-7 minutes. Add the ground meat and brown it, breaking it up with a spoon. Stir in the garlic, tomato puree, canned tomatoes, and beef stock. Season and simmer for at least 30 minutes, or longer for a richer flavor.
  2. Prepare the Bechamel: In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for a minute to form a roux. Gradually pour in the milk, whisking constantly to create a smooth sauce. Bring to a simmer, whisking, until the sauce thickens. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  3. Assemble the Lasagne:
    • Preheat the oven to 200C (400F)
    • Spread a thin layer of Ragu on the baking dish
    • Place a layer of lasagne sheets over the sauce
    • Top with a layer of Bechamel sauce, and then another layer of Ragu
    • Repeat the layers, starting with lasagne sheets, until all ingredients are used. Finish with a final layer of Bechamel sauce, and cover the top with the shredded Mozarella and Parmesan
  4. Bake: Bake for 30-45 minutes, or until the top is golden-brown and bubbling and a knife can easily slide through the pasta. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
